Historical Background

The real-life circumstances surrounding the myth



Norse mythology comes from the North Germanic people.

It stems from Norse Paganism. There are numerous Gods,

including Odin, the crafty knowlege god, and Freyja, the vengeful,

skiing goddess. The norse mythyology's cosmos has nine worlds,

and units of time are often personified as Deities.

Thor was the most popular God among the ancient Norse common men.



Scholars have studied Norse Mythology since the 17th century, when

the ancient texts were discovered and translated.

Thanks to the romanticist Viking Revival, elements of

the Norse Mythology (most notably, Thor) can be found throughout

American popular culture. The day "Thursday" on the modern weekly

calendar is said to be derived from "Thor's Day."
